Round-robin
•RR, also known as time slicing
•Preemptive using a clock interrupt generated at periodic 
intervals
•If interrupt occurs, running process is pre-empted (each 
process is given a time quantum, usually 10-100msec to use 
CPI)
•No job is allocated the CPU for more than one time 
quantum.
•If there are n processes in the ready queue and the time 
quantum is q, then each process gets 1/n of the CPU time in 
chunks of at most q time units at once.
•No process waits more than (n-1)q  time units.
