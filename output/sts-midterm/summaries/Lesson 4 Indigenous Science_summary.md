## INDIGENOUS SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY IN THE PHILIPPINES

### Introduction

Early Filipinos developed tools and unique ways of understanding the world. This traditional system of knowledge is known as **Indigenous Knowledge**, which forms the basis of **Indigenous Science**.

### What is Indigenous Knowledge (IK)?

*   **Definition:** Indigenous Knowledge refers to the knowledge systems developed by a specific community.
*   **Distinction:** It is distinct from what is generally called "modern" or "scientific" knowledge.

### Examples of Indigenous Knowledge and Practices

Indigenous people practice and pass down knowledge in various areas, including:

*   **Weather Prediction:** Using observations of animal behavior and celestial bodies to forecast weather and seasons.
*   **Herbal Medicine:** Knowledge and use of plants for medicinal purposes.
*   **Food Preservation:** Techniques for preserving food.
*   **Classification Systems:** Grouping plants and animals based on cultural properties.
*   **Seed Management:** Preserving and selecting quality seeds for planting.
*   **Indigenous Technology:** Utilizing traditional tools and methods in daily life.

### Section 3: Indigenous Fermented Beverages

Several traditional alcoholic beverages are produced using indigenous methods and local ingredients:

*   **Tuba**
    *   **Description:** Coconut wine. In the Visayas, it's mixed with *barok* (reddish mangrove bark), giving it a red color, often called "coconut red wine."
    *   **Aliases:** Coconut Red Wine, Coconut Toddy, Jungle Wine, Bahal, Bahalina.
*   **Lambanog**
    *   **Description:** Philippine Coconut Wine, known for its high strength (80-90 proof). Made from pure coconut sap, resulting in an almost colorless or milky white liquid ("coconut vodka").
    *   **Main Production Area:** Quezon Province, Philippines.
    *   **Key Difference from Tuba (Visayas):** Lambanog is typically pure sap (clear/white), while Visayan Tuba is red due to *barok*.
*   **Basi**
    *   **Description:** Fermented alcoholic beverage made from sugarcane.
    *   **Origin:** Ilocos region, Northern Luzon, Philippines.
*   **Laksoy**
    *   **Description:** Liquor distilled from the sap of a nipa palm.
    *   **Origin:** Widely made and available in Caraga, Mindanao.
*   **Tapuy**
    *   **Description:** Rice wine, a naturally fermented alcoholic beverage made from cooked glutinous rice.
    *   **Development:** A result of research and development by the Philippine Rice Research Institute (PhilRice).
    *   **Alias:** PhilRice Tapuy.
*   **Other Examples:** Bignay / Bugnay (Wild Berry Wine), Coffee Wine, Strawberry wine.

**What is Indigenous Science?**

*   **Definition:** Indigenous science is the process by which Indigenous people develop their empirical knowledge of their natural environment.
*   **Scope (Cajete, 2004):** It encompasses everything from metaphysics and philosophy to various practical technologies practiced by Indigenous peoples, both historically and in the present day.

### **Components of Indigenous Science**

Indigenous Science integrates three key aspects:

#### **1. Science Process Skills**

Indigenous Science utilizes various science process skills, including:

*   Observing
*   Comparing
*   Classifying
*   Measuring
*   Problem-solving
*   Inferring
*   Communicating
*   Predicting

#### **2. Community Cultures and Values**

Indigenous Science is deeply rooted in and guided by specific cultural beliefs and community values. These include:

*   **Land as Source of Life:** The land is considered a precious gift and the origin of life.
*   **Reverence for "Mother Earth":** The Earth is revered as the origin of identity for the people.
*   **Interconnectedness:** All living and non-living things are seen as interconnected and interdependent.
*   **Stewardship:** Humans are stewards or trustees of the land and natural resources, with a responsibility to preserve them.
*   **Respect for Nature:** Nature is a friend that requires respect and proper care.

#### **3. Traditional Knowledge**

Indigenous Science encompasses traditional knowledge systems practiced and valued by communities. Examples include:

*   **Ethno-biology:** Traditional knowledge of plants, animals, and ecosystems.
*   **Ethno-medicine:** Traditional healing practices and knowledge of medicinal plants.
*   **Indigenous Farming Methods:** Traditional agricultural techniques.
*   **Folk Astronomy:** Traditional knowledge of celestial bodies and their patterns.
